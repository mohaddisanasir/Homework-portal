FOLLOW THESE GUIDELINES: Project: Baking Soda Volcano Objective: To observe a chemical reaction between baking soda and vinegar.
  • Materials Needed:
  • Small plastic bottle or cup
  • Baking soda (about 2 tablespoons)
  • Vinegar (about 1/2 cup)
  • Dish or tray (to catch the mess)
  • Food coloring (optional)
  • Funnel (optional)
Steps: Set Up:
  1. Place the small bottle or cup in the center of the dish or tray to catch any overflow. Add Baking Soda
  2. Using a funnel (if you have one), pour about 2 tablespoons of baking soda into the bottle. Add Food Coloring (Optional)
  3. If you want colored lava, add a few drops of food coloring to the baking soda. Prepare for Eruption
  4. Pour about 1/2 cup of vinegar into the bottle with the baking soda. Watch the Eruption
  5. Stand back and watch the volcano erupt! The mixture will bubble and fizz, resembling lava flowing out. Clean Up
  6. Once the eruption is done, clean up any mess with a cloth or paper towels
    7. . Conclusion: Discuss what happened. Why did the eruption occur? Explain that the baking soda (a base) reacts with the vinegar (an acid) to produce carbon dioxide gas, creating the fizzing action. This project is simple, exciting, and a great way to learn about chemical reactions!
